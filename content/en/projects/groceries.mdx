Rural Romanian widows and elderly neighbors typically live on a state pension of a few hundred dollars a month. That pays the heat and electricity. It does not always pay the groceries.

The groceries ministry sits in the gap. Once a month — twice a month for the most stretched households — a local partner picks up a standard kit of bread, oil, sugar, flour, milk, eggs, and a small portion of meat or canned protein, and delivers it to families on a list the local pastors keep. No one has to ask in person each time. The board reviews the list quarterly, adds names that come up, and removes names whose situations have improved.

## How grocery delivery works

The pastor in each village keeps the working list. A trusted in-country partner does the shopping at a local grocery store using a transferred budget — typically $30 to $60 per household per visit — and delivers in person. We pay the grocer directly and reimburse the partner's fuel; no cash changes hands at the household.

## Give to the Groceries fund

A monthly gift to the Groceries fund replenishes the kits before each delivery cycle. $25 covers a household for a week; $50 stretches to a month for one widow.
